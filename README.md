## Assignment Summary:

For this project, students will use Python code (Pandas, scikit-learn, statsmodels, etc.) to setup, execute, and interpret a line of inquiry based on computer vision methods. Specifically, teams of 3-4 students will extend either of our CV homework assignments by either pursuing deeper analysis of the same dataset, or by extending the assignment's core methods to a new problem context, domain area, or dataset, etc. Students will be evaluated based on the quality of their technical implementations; the depth of their inquiry; their use of a deep learning component, and their success at communicating their work in written and visual forms.

## Assignment Purpose:

This assignment asks students to fulfill the following learning goals:

1. Conceptualize and execute original research in descriptive data analytics while using a computer vision approach
2. Use Python (including Pandas, scikit-learn, opencv, tensorflow, etc.) to implement your line of inquiry
3. Demonstrate your ability to conduct computer vision methods, to generate results, and to interpret those results effectively
4. Use written and visual forms of communication effectively in service of the project
5. Demonstrate effective teamwork supported by version control software (Github)

These learning goals are important to our class because descriptive data analytics as we teach it at Denison is closely tied to modeling for insights, natural language processing, and computer vision. These focal areas are, in turn, crucial aspects of data analytics as a whole.  

## Topic Selection:

The topic ideas below are all examples of the idea of extending a modes inquiry from the computer vision homework assignments by pursuing a deeper analysis. You can select one of these research questions and run with it, or propose your own alternative. 

1. Extend our work on classifying Calvin and Hobbes by using a Visual-Bag-of-Words model, a CNN, a Vision Transformer (ViT), etc.
2. Train and test an object detection approach to the Calvin and Hobbes task (RNN) that can find both Calvin and Hobbes on the page
3. Implement a computer vision model that can classify or identify Calvin and Hobbes in black and white images 
4. Reproduce and __extend__ the road condition analysis from Brewer et al. in some meaningful way
5. Reproduce and __extend__ the pedestrian tracking analysis from Thomas et al. in some meaningful way
6. Design a bird ensemble model that can out probabilities from multiple classifiers and predict a novel bird species (one not in the training data) by describing its similarity to other species in the training data.

## Assignment Details:

Each project submission will mix Python code and markdown text to provide a coherent narrative of your project. You will create it as a Marimo Notebook file and submit it in both `.py` and `.html` formats. Submissions should adhere to the following formatting guidelines: 

| Component     | Description                                              |
|:--------------|:---------------------------------------------------------|
| Title                   | Brief and informative, gives some idea of your topic area. |
| Introduction            | Include core background information and ethical considerations relevant to initial data collection and contemporary use of the data. Articulate a research question that can be addressed with computational, data-driven analysis. Describe your focal data set and research design. |
| Methods        | Describe your methods and why they are appropriate for the research question. Describe the strengths and weaknesses of all methods to be used. Cite sources as needed to justify how the methods are to be used and interpreted. |
| Results        | Fully report your results, including the relevant predictive power, statistical significance, and/or validity of all implemented models. Discuss coefficients where relevant. Use tables and figures as needed. |
| Interpretation       |  This section should include a fully developed interpretation that is consistent with the results and clearly addresses the research question. Discuss here any major caveats or limitations to the interpretation, the extent to which it can be generalized, and how it might be extended by further research. |
| Uses of Python          | Take a step back and analyze your own use of code. Includes table of technical dependencies. Provide some rationale for choices you’ve made. Considerations may include performance, human readability, code dependencies, and reproducibility.       |
| References              | List all works cited in the data guide. Use proper APA format. |

## Assessment Criteria

Submissions will be evaluated on technical content; research and writing; and how well the submission comes together as a whole. The following descriptive rubric will be used when grading.

### Technical Aspects 

- attention is paid to the complexities of the source data (missing data, coded values, sample weighting, etc.)
- code runs without errors and is written in a reasonably efficient and human-readable way
- data visualizations are properly coded and sufficiently polished
- technical components are met and described in the 'Uses of Code' section

### Written and Visual Communication 

- the project is well organized, flows logically, and follows all formatting guidelines
- the submission's use of language is appropriate for a well-informed, less technical reader (includes sufficient attention to proofreading)
- data visualizations are used effectively for multiple aspects of the project
- submission has effective introduction, methods, results, and interpretation sections

### Big Picture 

- all materials are turned in on time and in the right place
- assignment directions are followed, and all required components are included in the submission
- proper documentation and version control methods are used to facilitate collaboration and reproducibility
- the submission provides sufficient details or points to supplementary materials that make the research reproducible (e.g. detailed footnotes, appendices, links to GitHub, etc.)
- submitted materials synthesize aspects of the course to pursue a clear line of descriptive data analytics inquiry
